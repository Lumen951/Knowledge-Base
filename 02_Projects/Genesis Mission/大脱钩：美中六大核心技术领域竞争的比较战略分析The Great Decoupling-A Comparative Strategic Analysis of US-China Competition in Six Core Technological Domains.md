

## Executive Summary: The Strategic Balance of Power

## 执行摘要：战略力量平衡

The competition between the United States and the People's Republic of China (PRC) across six core strategic domains—Advanced Manufacturing, Biotechnology, Critical Materials, Nuclear Energy, Quantum Information Science (QIS), and Semiconductors—is rapidly redefining global technological and economic structures. The current balance of power reveals a contest where the US retains a distinct lead in foundational research, chip design, intellectual property (IP), and private sector innovation, while China demonstrates overwhelming dominance in manufacturing scale, critical materials processing, and the rapid, top-down deployment of industrial capacity.

美国与中华人民共和国（PRC）在六大核心战略领域——先进制造、生物技术、关键材料、核能、量子信息科学 (QIS) 和半导体——之间的竞争，正在迅速重塑全球技术和经济结构。目前的权力平衡显示出一场博弈：美国在基础研究、芯片设计、知识产权 (IP) 和私营部门创新方面保持显著领先，而中国在制造规模、关键材料加工以及工业产能的快速自上而下部署方面表现出压倒性的优势。

The competition is centered on the pursuit of technological hegemony in the Fourth Industrial Revolution.[1] Beijing leverages its state-led model—exemplified by massive, targeted capital injection, superior industrial policies (such as Made in China 2025), and control over foundational supply chains—to move up the global value chain rapidly.[1, 2] Washington employs a strategy combining export controls (Guardrails) to restrict access to advanced technologies, coupled with targeted domestic subsidies (CHIPS Act) to restore resilience and capacity.[3, 4]

这场竞争的核心是对第四次工业革命技术霸权的追求。[1] 北京利用其国家主导模式——以大规模、定向的资本注入、卓越的产业政策（如“中国制造2025”）以及对基础供应链的控制为代表——迅速向全球价值链上游移动。[1, 2] 华盛顿则采取了一种结合出口管制（护栏）以限制对先进技术的获取，并辅以定向国内补贴（《芯片法案》）以恢复韧性和产能的战略。[3, 4]

A critical finding is the pervasive reliance on high-performance computing (HPC) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) as the accelerant across all sectors, making the availability of advanced compute capacity, and the stable, cheap energy (Nuclear/Fusion) required to power it, a primary constraint and strategic differentiator.[5, 6] The technological rivalry has solidified the trajectory toward a fragmented global system, characterized by the emergence of "One World, Two Systems" in high-tech supply chains.[7]

一个关键发现是，所有行业都普遍依赖高性能计算 (HPC) 和人工智能 (AI) 作为加速器，这使得先进算力的可用性，以及为其供电所需的稳定、廉价的能源（核能/聚变能），成为主要的制约因素和战略差异化因素。[5, 6] 技术竞争巩固了全球体系走向碎片化的趋势，其特征是高科技供应链中出现了“同一个世界，两套系统”。[7]

Key areas of vulnerability for the US include critical materials refining capacity and deep manufacturing know-how, while China remains critically dependent on US and allied control over advanced semiconductor manufacturing equipment and leading-edge chip design.[8, 9]

美国的关键脆弱领域包括关键材料的精炼能力和深厚的制造技术诀窍，而中国则在先进半导体制造设备和前沿芯片设计方面严重依赖美国及其盟友的控制。[8, 9]

## Section 1: Context and Strategic Framework

## 第一部分：背景与战略框架

### 1.1 Defining the Contest: The Fourth Industrial Revolution and Technological Hegemony

### 1.1 定义竞争：第四次工业革命与技术霸权

The rivalry between the US and China is fundamentally a strategic contest for dominance in the technologies central to the Fourth Industrial Revolution, encompassing sectors like 5G, advanced semiconductors, artificial intelligence, and quantum computing.[3] Both nations view leadership in these sectors as the foundational prerequisite for achieving long-term global economic and geopolitical superiority.[1]

美中之间的竞争从根本上讲是一场争夺第四次工业革命核心技术主导权的战略竞赛，涵盖5G、先进半导体、人工智能和量子计算等领域。[3] 两国都将这些领域的领导地位视为实现长期全球经济和地缘政治优势的基础前提。[1]

China’s leadership frames this contest through a historical lens, believing that seizing control of the next major industrial revolution, much like the US did previously, will establish a new era of global influence.[1] Beijing cites four main advantages in this pursuit: first, heavy, coordinated investment in R&D, with China’s total R&D spending now estimated to be roughly equivalent to that of the US [1]; second, institutional superiority rooted in top-down industrial policies designed to support national ambitions (e.g., Made in China 2025); third, established manufacturing prowess and centrality within global supply chains; and fourth, a robust operation dedicated to setting future global technology standards.[1] The substantial government investment is particularly noticeable in nascent but critical sectors; for example, China's estimated annual spending on certain key technologies is more than ten times what the US allocates, alongside plans for building large-scale infrastructure like the $10 billion National Laboratory for Quantum Information Sciences.[1]

中国领导层通过历史视角来看待这场竞争，认为掌握下一次重大工业革命的控制权——就像美国此前所做的那样——将确立全球影响力的新时代。[1] 北京在此追求中列举了四大主要优势：首先是大量、协调的研发投资，目前中国的研发总支出估计与美国大致相当 [1]；其次是植根于旨在支持国家雄心的自上而下的产业政策（如“中国制造2025”）的制度优势；第三是既有的制造实力及其在全球供应链中的中心地位；第四是致力于制定未来全球技术标准的强有力行动。[1] 这种巨大的政府投资在处于萌芽状态但至关重要的领域尤为明显；例如，据估计中国在某些关键技术上的年度支出是美国拨款的十倍以上，同时还计划建设大规模基础设施，如耗资100亿美元的国家量子信息科学实验室。[1]

### 1.2 Foundational Policy Mechanisms: The US "Guardrails" Strategy vs. China’s State Capitalism Model

### 1.2 基础政策机制：美国的“护栏”战略 vs. 中国的国家资本主义模式

The strategic approaches employed by Washington and Beijing reflect their deeply divergent political and economic systems.

华盛顿和北京采取的战略方针反映了其深刻分歧的政治和经济体制。

The US Strategy: Containment and Resilience

美国战略：遏制与韧性

The US strategy is dual-pronged: reducing reliance on Chinese technology in areas posing national security risks and preventing the transfer of critical American technologies to China.[3] Key policy tools include stringent export controls, particularly those restricting the acquisition of advanced AI chips and sophisticated semiconductor manufacturing equipment.[5, 9] Domestically, legislation such as the CHIPS and Science Act and the Inflation Reduction Act aims to enhance domestic capacity and secure supply chains.[4, 10] The CHIPS Act, for instance, provides $52.7 billion in subsidies but includes "guardrail clauses" specifically aimed at restricting investments in advanced chip manufacturing in China by recipients, directly challenging China’s expansionist aims.[4, 11]

美国的战略是双管齐下的：在构成国家安全风险的领域减少对中国技术的依赖，并防止关键的美国技术向中国转移。[3] 主要的政策工具包括严格的出口管制，特别是限制获取先进AI芯片和尖端半导体制造设备的管制。[5, 9] 在国内，诸如《芯片与科学法案》和《通胀削减法案》等立法旨在增强国内能力并确保供应链安全。[4, 10] 例如，《芯片法案》提供527亿美元的补贴，但包含了“护栏条款”，专门旨在限制受资助者在中国投资先进芯片制造，直接挑战了中国的扩张目标。[4, 11]

China’s Strategy: Self-Sufficiency and Dominance

中国战略：自给自足与主导地位

China’s strategy is driven by the state-led industrial policy framework, most prominently the Made in China 2025 (MIC 2025) program and the 14th Five Year Plan.[2, 12, 13] MIC 2025, released in 2015, outlines a ten-year plan to update China's manufacturing base and achieve dominance in ten high-tech industries, including next-generation IT, advanced robotics, new energy vehicles, and biopharma.[2, 12] This policy utilizes government subsidies, mobilizes state-owned enterprises (SOEs), and pursues aggressive intellectual property acquisition to quickly catch up with and surpass Western technological prowess.[2] China’s primary objective is to achieve high domestic market share targets and move decisively up the global value chain, even as it faces vulnerabilities imposed by foreign export controls on leading-edge equipment and chips.[5, 9]

中国的战略由国家主导的产业政策框架驱动，其中最著名的是“中国制造2025”（MIC 2025）计划和“十四五”规划。[2, 12, 13] 2015年发布的“中国制造2025”概述了一项十年计划，旨在更新中国的制造基础，并在包括下一代信息技术、先进机器人、新能源汽车和生物医药在内的十大高科技产业中实现主导地位。[2, 12] 该政策利用政府补贴、动员国有企业 (SOE)，并推行激进的知识产权获取策略，以迅速追赶并超越西方的技术实力。[2] 中国的主要目标是实现较高的国内市场份额目标，并果断地向全球价值链上游移动，即使面临外国对前沿设备和芯片出口管制所带来的脆弱性。[5, 9]

### 1.3 The Cross-Cutting Enabler: AI and HPC as the Accelerant

### 1.3 跨领域赋能者：AI 和 HPC 作为加速器

Artificial intelligence and high-performance computing (HPC) serve as the central technological accelerant across all six strategic domains. AI drives demand for advanced, high-performance chips, such as GPUs and specialized AI accelerators, making the global AI leadership race fundamentally a race for these advanced semiconductors.[17]

人工智能和高性能计算 (HPC) 是跨越所有六大战略领域的核心技术加速器。AI 推动了对先进高性能芯片（如 GPU 和专用 AI 加速器）的需求，使得全球 AI 领导地位的竞争从根本上变成了对这些先进半导体的竞争。[17]

The application of AI is instrumental in accelerating complex scientific processes, including drug discovery [18] and optimizing manufacturing workflows.[10] For example, AI techniques are revolutionizing advanced manufacturing by allowing the design and construction of products to become more akin to software design in intelligent factories.[10]

AI 的应用在加速复杂科学过程中发挥着关键作用，包括药物发现 [18] 和优化制造工作流程。[10] 例如，AI 技术正在彻底改变先进制造业，使得智能工厂中的产品设计和构建变得更类似于软件设计。[10]

A key strategic understanding in this rivalry is that the primary constraint on both the development of advanced AI models and the resulting scientific and industrial breakthroughs is access to massive compute capacity.[5, 17] The United States currently maintains an advantage in total compute capacity, owning significantly more advanced AI chips.[5] However, this advantage is being strategically targeted by China, confirming that the competition over compute is the most consequential driver shaping the global AI ecosystem and economic transformation.[5]

这场竞争中的一个关键战略共识是，先进 AI 模型的开发以及随之而来的科学和工业突破的主要制约因素，是获取海量算力的能力。[5, 17] 美国目前在总算力方面保持优势，拥有明显更多的先进 AI 芯片。[5] 然而，这一优势正成为中国的战略目标，这证实了对算力的争夺是塑造全球 AI 生态系统和经济转型的最重要驱动力。[5]

## Section 2: Advanced Manufacturing (4.1.1)

## 第二部分：先进制造 (4.1.1)

Advanced Manufacturing, defined by the application of AI and other emerging technologies to industrial processes, represents a critical technology battleground.[10]

先进制造，定义为 AI 和其他新兴技术在工业过程中的应用，代表了一个关键的技术战场。[10]

### 2.1 Scientific Frontiers: AI-Driven Materials Discovery and Process Simulation Efficiency

### 2.1 科学前沿：AI 驱动的材料发现与工艺模拟效率

The incorporation of AI techniques marks a paradigm shift in materials science and manufacturing process development. AI is used to accelerate R&D by predicting material properties, optimizing production lines, and simulating complex processes.[10, 18] For instance, AI algorithms, combined with advances in computational power and sensor technology, enable the creation of "intelligent factories".[10]

AI 技术的引入标志着材料科学和制造工艺开发的范式转变。AI 被用于通过预测材料特性、优化生产线和模拟复杂过程来加速研发。[10, 18] 例如，AI 算法结合计算能力和传感器技术的进步，使得“智能工厂”的创建成为可能。[10]

In drug discovery, a highly specialized form of advanced manufacturing, AI models have demonstrated the ability to reduce multiyear discovery processes down to approximately 18 months, dramatically cutting cost and time by predicting drug toxicity, bioactivity, and optimizing designs.[13, 18] These methods, utilizing large data resources and molecular representation schemes, are transferable to the design and synthesis of novel industrial materials. The shift to a software-defined factory, enabled by the proliferation of cheap sensors and rapid improvements in computation, facilitates tighter feedback loops between design, engineering, and physical production, which is crucial for accelerating innovation.[10]

在药物发现这一高度专业化的先进制造形式中，AI 模型已展示出将多年的发现过程缩短至大约 18 个月的能力，通过预测药物毒性、生物活性并优化设计，大幅降低了成本和时间。[13, 18] 这些利用大数据资源和分子表征方案的方法，可转移用于新型工业材料的设计和合成。廉价传感器的普及和计算能力的快速提升促成了向软件定义工厂的转变，这促进了设计、工程和物理生产之间更紧密的反馈循环，对于加速创新至关重要。[10]

### 2.2 Market & Corporate Dominance: Analyzing the Top-Down vs. Bottom-Up Model

### 2.2 市场与企业主导地位：分析自上而下与自下而上的模式

The US and China exhibit fundamental differences in their approach to the manufacturing value chain, leading to structural advantages and vulnerabilities.

美国和中国在处理制造价值链的方式上表现出根本性差异，导致了结构性的优势和脆弱性。

US Top-Down Focus and Vulnerability

美国的自上而下侧重与脆弱性

U.S. technology firms have historically concentrated on the upper, highest-profit ends of the value chain: system design, software, IP, and customer experience. This "top-down" approach, while successful in dominating the most profitable market segments, created an inherent reliance on manufacturing capacity primarily located in Asia.[16] This outsourcing trend, which began in the 1980s to reduce costs, resulted in unintended negative consequences, including supply chain dependencies, skill shortages, and vulnerability to intentionally imposed embargoes.[10, 16]

美国科技公司历来集中在价值链顶端、利润最高的部分：系统设计、软件、知识产权和客户体验。这种“自上而下”的方法虽然在主导最赚钱的细分市场方面取得了成功，但也造成了对主要位于亚洲的制造能力的内在依赖。[16] 这种始于 1980 年代旨在降低成本的外包趋势，导致了意想不到的负面后果，包括供应链依赖、技能短缺以及面对蓄意禁运时的脆弱性。[10, 16]

China's Bottom-Up Prowess

中国的自下而上实力

China, conversely, adopted a "bottom-up" strategy through its MIC 2025 policy.[16] This involved starting with high-volume, often low-margin production and systematically moving up the value chain. This strategy, initially modest, allowed Chinese businesses to accumulate scale, skills, ambition, and profitability over time.[16] This approach has been highly successful: China has claimed to have met or exceeded 86 percent of its ambitious MIC 2025 targets in key priority sectors, including robotics and New Energy Vehicles (NEVs).[12, 13]

相反，中国通过其 MIC 2025 政策采取了“自下而上”的战略。[16] 这涉及从大批量、通常是低利润的生产开始，系统地向价值链上游移动。这一战略起初并不起眼，但让中国企业随着时间的推移积累了规模、技能、雄心和盈利能力。[16] 这种方法非常成功：中国声称在关键优先领域（包括机器人和新能源汽车 NEV）已达到或超过了其雄心勃勃的 MIC 2025 目标的 86%。[12, 13]

### 2.3 Government Policy: Incentives for Reshoring vs. State-Owned Enterprise Mobilization

### 2.3 政府政策：回流激励 vs. 国有企业动员

U.S. policy explicitly aims to rebuild the domestic advanced manufacturing ecosystem, recognizing that this capability—encompassing both capacity and know-how—is a national security imperative.[10] Landmark legislation, including the CHIPS Act and the IRA, represents the starting point for this accelerated innovation and deployment.[10]

美国政策明确旨在重建国内先进制造生态系统，认识到这一能力——包括产能和专有技术——是国家安全的必要条件。[10] 标志性立法，包括《芯片法案》和《通胀削减法案》(IRA)，代表了这一加速创新和部署的起点。[10]

In the PRC, MIC 2025 provides the policy architecture, using state subsidies and mobilization of SOEs to achieve aggressive domestic market share targets. For instance, goals were set to achieve 70 percent domestic market share for industrial robotics and 80 percent for EVs and batteries.[13] The state-led framework provides coherent direction and capital not typically available in market-driven economies.

在中华人民共和国，MIC 2025 提供了政策架构，利用国家补贴和动员国有企业来实现激进的国内市场份额目标。例如，设定了工业机器人国内市场份额达到 70%、电动汽车和电池达到 80% 的目标。[13] 这种国家主导的框架提供了市场驱动型经济体中通常不具备的连贯方向和资本。

### 2.4 Competition and Cooperation: The Challenge of Restoring Capacity

### 2.4 竞争与合作：恢复产能的挑战

The effort by the United States to reduce its deep manufacturing dependencies faces a structural hurdle described by strategic analysts as the Innovator’s Dilemma, but applied in reverse.[16] Historical evidence shows it is far easier for companies and nations operating from the bottom (manufacturing base) to move upward (design/IP) than it is for those at the top (design/IP) to move downward to restore manufacturing depth.[16] Moving down the value chain often stems from necessity rather than enthusiasm, typically resulting in lower levels of confidence, requisite know-how, and profitability compared to the incremental, bottom-up growth achieved by competitors like China.[16]

美国为减少其深层制造业依赖所做的努力面临着一个被战略分析师描述为“创新者窘境”（反向应用）的结构性障碍。[16] 历史证据表明，对于从底部（制造基础）运营的公司和国家来说，向上移动（设计/IP）要比处于顶部（设计/IP）的公司和国家向下移动以恢复制造深度容易得多。[16] 向价值链下游移动往往源于必要性而非热情，通常导致信心、必要的技术诀窍和盈利能力水平低于像中国这样的竞争对手通过渐进式、自下而上增长所达到的水平。[16]

Furthermore, the outsourcing trend did not just transfer jobs; it severed the crucial "tight feedback loops" between design engineers and production capacity. The co-location of production capacity with a nation's innovation ecosystem is essential for fostering rapid, iterative innovation.[10] Thus, rebuilding the US advanced manufacturing ecosystem is not solely about cost reduction or supply chain security, but a prerequisite for sustained technological innovation itself.[10] Recognizing this structural difficulty confirms that reducing manufacturing dependencies in strategic industries should be the most important policy priority for the US, potentially superseding other goals such as restricting exports or altering China's business practices.[16]

此外，外包趋势不仅转移了工作岗位；它还切断了设计工程师与生产能力之间至关重要的“紧密反馈循环”。生产能力与国家创新生态系统的共同定位对于促进快速、迭代的创新至关重要。[10] 因此，重建美国先进制造生态系统不仅仅是为了降低成本或供应链安全，而是持续技术创新本身的前提条件。[10] 认识到这一结构性困难证实了，减少战略产业的制造业依赖应成为美国最重要的政策优先事项，可能优先于限制出口或改变中国商业惯例等其他目标。[16]

## Section 3: Biotechnology and Life Sciences (4.1.2)

## 第三部分：生物技术与生命科学 (4.1.2)

Biotechnology, encompassing the acceleration of new therapy discovery and bioengineering breakthroughs, is widely recognized by both the US and China as a strategic emerging industry integral to both national defense and future innovation.[13]

生物技术，涵盖新疗法发现的加速和生物工程突破，被美中两国广泛公认为对国防和未来创新不可或缺的战略性新兴产业。[13]

### 3.1 Scientific Breakthroughs: AI Foundation Models and Efficiency Gains in Drug Discovery

### 3.1 科学突破：AI 基础模型与药物发现的效率提升

The frontier of biotechnology research is increasingly defined by its convergence with artificial intelligence. AI models are revolutionizing the efficiency of the life science R&D pipeline.[18] These techniques, which involve complex molecular representation schemes and large accessible data resources, allow for rapid prediction of drug properties, toxicity, and target interaction.[18]

生物技术研究的前沿越来越被其与人工智能的融合所定义。AI 模型正在彻底改变生命科学研发管线的效率。[18] 这些涉及复杂分子表征方案和大量可访问数据资源的技术，使得快速预测药物特性、毒性和靶点相互作用成为可能。[18]

China has made significant strides in this area, exemplified by firms such as BioMap in Beijing, which claims to have developed an AI Foundation Model exceeding 100 billion parameters specifically to accelerate drug discovery, aiming to drastically cut the traditional multiyear discovery timeframe.[13]

中国在该领域取得了长足进步，北京的百图生科 (BioMap) 等公司就是例证，该公司声称已开发出一个超过 1000 亿参数的 AI 基础模型，专门用于加速药物发现，旨在大幅缩减传统的多年发现时间框架。[13]

A critical element of the AI-biotech nexus is the quality and volume of biological data. US national security agencies express serious concerns regarding China’s generation of high-quality biological data intended for AI development, recognizing the competitive advantage it confers.[13, 19]

AI 与生物技术结合的一个关键要素是生物数据的质量和数量。美国国家安全机构对中国生成用于 AI 开发的高质量生物数据表示严重关切，认识到这所赋予的竞争优势。[13, 19]

### 3.2 Market Structure: US Investment Ecosystem vs. China's Scale and Manufacturing Expertise

### 3.2 市场结构：美国的投资生态系统 vs. 中国的规模与制造专长

The US retains its position as the global leader in generating novel drugs and foundational biological innovation, largely sustained by its unique drug pricing structure that generates the necessary high profit margins to fund decades-long, high-risk R&D cycles.[19]

美国保持着其在产生新药和基础生物创新方面的全球领导地位，这主要得益于其独特的药物定价结构，该结构产生了必要的高利润率，以资助长达数十年、高风险的研发生命周期。[19]

However, China has rapidly established itself as a robust innovation engine, leveraging its deep pool of chemists and biologists alongside production expertise.[19] This capability has translated into a profound global supply chain dependence. Surveys conducted in 2024 revealed that between 74% and 79% of US biopharmaceutical companies rely on China-based manufacturers for pre-clinical and clinical services, a dependency relationship that mirrors China’s entrenched dominance in traditional manufacturing sectors.[19, 20] This reliance is increasing, evidenced by large US and European pharmaceutical companies significantly increasing licensing and merger and acquisition (M&A) activities in China, with 144 deals recorded in 2023–2024, up from 104 in the preceding two years.[19]

然而，中国利用其深厚的化学家和生物学家人才库以及生产专长，已迅速确立了自己作为强劲创新引擎的地位。[19] 这种能力已转化为深刻的全球供应链依赖。2024 年进行的调查显示，74% 到 79% 的美国生物制药公司依赖中国制造商提供临床前和临床服务，这种依赖关系反映了中国在传统制造领域根深蒂固的主导地位。[19, 20] 这种依赖正在增加，大型美国和欧洲制药公司在中国显著增加的许可和并购 (M&A) 活动就是证明，2023-2024 年记录了 144 笔交易，高于前两年的 104 笔。[19]

### 3.3 Regulatory Landscape: Comparative Analysis of FDA Rigor and NMPA Rapid Evolution

### 3.3 监管格局：FDA 的严格性与 NMPA 的快速演变之比较分析

The regulatory environment presents a key divergence point. The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) provides a predictable, established, and highly rigorous pathway that acts as a global gold standard for biologics review and approval.[21] Historically, the FDA treated clinical trials originating from China with suspicion, sometimes rejecting high-profile cancer drugs due to questionable or fraudulent trial data, leading many US drugmakers to cease licensing late-stage drugs that relied solely on pivotal Chinese trials.[22]

监管环境呈现出一个关键的分歧点。美国食品药品监督管理局 (FDA) 提供了一条可预测的、确立的且高度严格的路径，作为生物制剂审查和批准的全球黄金标准。[21] 历史上，FDA 对源自中国的临床试验持怀疑态度，有时因试验数据可疑或欺诈而拒绝高调的抗癌药物，导致许多美国制药商停止许可仅依赖中国关键试验的晚期药物。[22]

In contrast, China’s National Medical Products Administration (NMPA) regulatory environment is rapidly evolving and maturing, offering both challenges and opportunities for global biopharmaceutical companies.[21] However, China's policy of aggressive domestic price controls, while reducing healthcare costs for its citizens, inherently limits the profit margins required to sustain long-term, deep biotech innovation within the country.[19]

相比之下，中国的国家药品监督管理局 (NMPA) 监管环境正在迅速演变和成熟，为全球生物制药公司带来了挑战与机遇。[21] 然而，中国激进的国内价格控制政策虽然降低了公民的医疗成本，但本质上限制了维持国内长期、深度生物技术创新所需的利润率。[19]

### 3.4 Competition and Geopolitical Risk: The Dependency Paradox

### 3.4 竞争与地缘政治风险：依赖悖论

The technological rivalry in biotechnology is characterized by a "dependency paradox." The US maintains its R&D leadership and profits, but its drug pipeline is deeply reliant on Chinese contract manufacturing and clinical services.[19] Conversely, despite its growing innovation engine, China’s ability to commercialize novel drugs globally remains contingent upon securing regulatory approval from established Western authorities, particularly the FDA.[19]

生物技术领域的技术竞争以“依赖悖论”为特征。美国保持着研发领导地位和利润，但其药物管线深深依赖中国的合同制造和临床服务。[19] 相反，尽管中国的创新引擎不断增长，但其在全球范围内商业化新药的能力仍然取决于获得成熟西方机构（特别是 FDA）的监管批准。[19]

This deep, mutual interdependence means that any unilateral policy push for rapid technological decoupling carries a high risk of disruption.[20] Strategic analysts recognize that cutting these ties prematurely would significantly slow innovation for both sides, complicating coordinated responses aimed at preserving US leadership.[20] The outcome of this biotechnological race will determine who shapes future agricultural production, global health, and biosecurity protocols.[19] Sustained US competition requires strategic investment in domestic capabilities to counter the inevitable quick catch-up by Chinese technology and scientists.[19]

这种深刻的相互依赖意味着，任何单方面推动快速技术脱钩的政策都带有极高的中断风险。[20] 战略分析师认识到，过早切断这些联系将显着减缓双方的创新，使旨在保持美国领导地位的协调应对措施变得复杂化。[20] 这场生物技术竞赛的结果将决定谁来塑造未来的农业生产、全球卫生和生物安全协议。[19] 美国要确保持续的竞争力，就需要对国内能力进行战略投资，以应对中国技术和科学家不可避免的快速追赶。[19]

## Section 4: Critical Materials and Supply Chain Security (4.1.3)

## 第四部分：关键材料与供应链安全 (4.1.3)

The domain of critical materials, particularly rare earth elements (REEs) and related processing, has transitioned from a niche industry challenge to a central instrument of geopolitical competition and national security.[8]

关键材料领域，特别是稀土元素 (REEs) 及相关加工，已从一个小众的行业挑战转变为地缘政治竞争和国家安全的核心工具。[8]

### 4.1 Geopolitical Dominance: China’s Strategic Control over Refining and Permanent Magnet Production

### 4.1 地缘政治主导地位：中国对精炼和永磁体生产的战略控制

China has secured a foundational strategic advantage by dominating the global processing chain for critical minerals. Following the famous dictum attributed to Deng Xiaoping, "The Middle East has oil, China has rare earths," Beijing realized the geopolitical value of REEs.[23] China currently refines 85% of the world's light REEs and holds overwhelming control over more than 99% of heavy REE refining capacity.[23]

中国通过主导关键矿产的全球加工链，确立了基础性的战略优势。遵循归于邓小平的那句名言“中东有石油，中国有稀土”，北京意识到了稀土元素的地缘政治价值。[23] 目前，中国精炼了全球 85% 的轻稀土，并拥有对超过 99% 重稀土精炼产能的压倒性控制权。[23]

This refining dominance translates directly into a chokehold on advanced technology production: China controls over 90% of global production of permanent magnets.[24] These magnets are indispensable components across all strategic sectors, powering electric vehicle (EV) motors, wind turbines, AI hardware, and, critically, sophisticated defense systems such as the F-35 fighter jet, Tomahawk missiles, and Virginia-class submarines.[23, 25] A single F-35 aircraft, for example, requires 418 kg of rare earths.[23]

这种精炼主导地位直接转化为对先进技术生产的扼制：中国控制着全球 90% 以上的永磁体生产。[24] 这些磁体是所有战略领域不可或缺的组件，为电动汽车 (EV) 电机、风力涡轮机、AI 硬件以及至关重要的尖端防御系统（如 F-35 战斗机、战斧导弹和弗吉尼亚级潜艇）提供动力。[23, 25] 例如，一架 F-35 飞机需要 418 公斤的稀土。[23]

### 4.2 Strategic Minerals Vulnerability: Assessing Dependency

### 4.2 战略矿产脆弱性：评估依赖度

The strategic vulnerability for the US extends beyond REEs to other core inputs for the future energy economy. Four strategic minerals—lithium, nickel, cobalt, and graphite—are foundational to lithium-ion technologies that power modern electronics, grid storage, and EVs.[8]

美国的战略脆弱性不仅限于稀土元素，还延伸到未来能源经济的其他核心投入。锂、镍、钴和石墨这四种战略矿物是锂离子技术的基础，为现代电子产品、电网存储和电动汽车提供动力。[8]

While raw reserves are geographically dispersed, the true strategic leverage lies in processing capacity. For instance, global refining capacity for lithium is almost entirely concentrated in just two countries, with China controlling approximately 65 percent.[8] The situation is even more acute for graphite, where China controls roughly 90 percent of the global supply chain, covering extraction, processing, and exports.[8] Securing access to these minerals is therefore deemed essential for national security and economic competitiveness.[8]

虽然原始储量在地理上是分散的，但真正的战略杠杆在于加工能力。例如，全球锂的精炼能力几乎完全集中在两个国家，其中中国控制了约 65%。[8] 石墨的情况更为严峻，中国控制着全球供应链的约 90%，涵盖开采、加工和出口。[8] 因此，确保获取这些矿物被认为对国家安全和经济竞争力至关重要。[8]

### 4.3 Policy Responses: US Diversification Strategy and Allied Frameworks

### 4.3 政策响应：美国的多元化战略与联盟框架

The US and its allies are urgently pursuing measures to break China’s stranglehold on supply.[25] This strategic focus has shifted from merely identifying domestic reserves to recognizing that true strategic advantage depends on building and securing access to adequate refining and processing capacity.[8] The real value in the supply chain is realized in processed products, not just raw materials.[8]

美国及其盟友正在紧迫地采取措施，以打破中国对供应的扼制。[25] 这一战略重点已从仅仅确定国内储量，转变为认识到真正的战略优势取决于建立并确保获得足够的精炼和加工能力。[8] 供应链的真正价值体现在加工产品中，而不仅仅是原材料。[8]

Governments in the US, Europe, Japan, and Australia are accelerating efforts through direct financing, tax incentives, and procurement guarantees to diversify supply chains.[25] For example, the US Department of Defense acquired a 15% stake in MP Materials, the largest US rare earths producer, to secure domestic supply.[25]

美国、欧洲、日本和澳大利亚政府正通过直接融资、税收优惠和采购担保来加速供应链多元化的努力。[25] 例如，美国国防部收购了美国最大的稀土生产商 MP Materials 15% 的股份，以确保国内供应。[25]

### 4.4 Escalation Dynamics: The Use and Impact of Export Controls

### 4.4 升级态势：出口管制的使用与影响

China has frequently used its rare earth leverage to counter Washington’s containment policies.[23, 27] This power was starkly illustrated in October when China announced export controls on critical minerals and related processing technologies, carrying enormous economic consequences for industries worldwide.[8, 28]

中国经常利用其稀土杠杆来反击华盛顿的遏制政策。[23, 27] 这种力量在 10 月份得到了鲜明的体现，当时中国宣布对关键矿产及相关加工技术实施出口管制，给全球各行业带来了巨大的经济后果。[8, 28]

In a subsequent diplomatic move, Beijing announced the temporary suspension of several newly implemented export controls in November 2025.[28] This measured de-escalation, framed as a confidence-building step, paused the tightening of licensing requirements for REEs, magnet materials, and specific dual-use materials like gallium and germanium, with the US-specific pause lasting until November 2026.[28] This temporary reprieve provides industries dependent on these materials a brief window to reassess sourcing strategies and prepare for the possibility that controls may be reimposed once geopolitical dynamics shift.[8, 28] This calculated use of trade pressure confirms China's strategic power in the materials domain and underscores the urgency for dependent nations to accelerate supply chain diversification.[25]

在随后的一项外交举措中，北京宣布于 2025 年 11 月暂停实施几项新近出台的出口管制。[28] 这种有分寸的降级被定性为建立信任的步骤，暂停了收紧对稀土、磁体材料以及镓和锗等特定军民两用材料的许可要求，其中针对美国的暂停期持续到 2026 年 11 月。[28] 这一暂时的缓解为依赖这些材料的行业提供了一个短暂的窗口，以重新评估采购策略，并为一旦地缘政治动态发生变化可能重新实施管制做好准备。[8, 28] 这种对贸易压力的精心算计的使用，证实了中国在材料领域的战略力量，并凸显了依赖国家加速供应链多元化的紧迫性。[25]

## Section 5: Nuclear Fission and Fusion Energy (4.1.4)

## 第五部分：核裂变与聚变能 (4.1.4)

Nuclear energy, encompassing both optimized fission systems and the race for commercial fusion, is vital for providing the reliable, low-carbon electricity necessary to power the next generation of energy-intensive industries, particularly high-performance computing and advanced manufacturing.[6, 29]

核能，涵盖优化的裂变系统和商业聚变竞赛，对于提供可靠的低碳电力至关重要，这是为下一代能源密集型产业（特别是高性能计算和先进制造）供电所必需的。[6, 29]

### 5.1 Scientific Acceleration: Status of Nuclear Fusion Commercialization

### 5.1 科学加速：核聚变商业化现状

Nuclear fusion, the elusive source of clean energy, has seen a rapid influx of private funding globally, driven in part by the accelerating power demands of AI.[6] While the US has traditionally led in fusion research, China has ramped up its efforts considerably. China is reportedly spending twice as much on fusion as the US and is building projects faster.[6]

核聚变这一难以捉摸的清洁能源来源，在全球范围内见证了私人资金的迅速涌入，部分原因是 AI 对电力需求的加速增长。[6] 虽然美国传统上在聚变研究方面处于领先地位，但中国已大大加强了力度。据报道，中国在聚变方面的支出是美国的两倍，并且项目建设速度更快。[6]

Strategic assessments suggest that China is on the verge of surpassing the US in the quest for commercial nuclear fusion.[30] The success of the nation that commercializes fusion first will depend not just on scientific breakthroughs, but on their ability to develop complex, large-scale supply chains and build fusion plants at an economic scale.[30] China's strategy appears to involve cornering the necessary supply chain and talent pool to reach commercialization ahead of the US.[6]

战略评估表明，中国在寻求商业核聚变方面即将超越美国。[30] 率先实现聚变商业化的国家的成功，不仅取决于科学突破，还取决于其开发复杂的大规模供应链以及以经济规模建设聚变电厂的能力。[30] 中国的战略似乎涉及垄断必要的供应链和人才库，以便在美国之前实现商业化。[6]

### 5.2 Fission System Deployment: China’s Scale and Advanced Reactor Leadership

### 5.2 裂变系统部署：中国的规模与先进反应堆领导地位

The US remains the world's largest nuclear power producer, with 94 operational reactors generating 97 GW(e).[29] However, China leads decisively in the construction and deployment of new nuclear capacity.

美国仍然是世界上最大的核电生产国，拥有 94 座运行中的反应堆，发电量为 97 GW(e)。[29] 然而，中国在新核能产能的建设和部署方面处于决定性的领先地位。

China currently has 29 reactors (29.6 GW(e)) under construction, representing more than the rest of the world’s nuclear construction capacity combined.[29, 31] China operates 57 reactors (55.3 GW(e)) as of November 2025, but the forward-looking strategy is highly ambitious: Beijing targets 200 GW by 2030 and envisions 500 GW of nuclear capacity by 2050.[32]

目前，中国有 29 座反应堆 (29.6 GW(e)) 在建，超过了世界其他地区核建产能的总和。[29, 31] 截至 2025 年 11 月，中国运行着 57 座反应堆 (55.3 GW(e))，但其前瞻性战略雄心勃勃：北京的目标是到 2030 年达到 200 GW，并设想到 2050 年核能装机容量达到 500 GW。[32]

Moreover, China is taking the lead in deploying advanced reactor technology. Its Linglong-1 (ACP-100) is the world's first commercial Small Modular Reactor (SMR) to pass the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) safety review, with grid connection expected by the end of 2025.[31] China is also deploying advanced systems like Fast Breeder Reactors (FBRs) and is slated to launch the world's first molten salt and thorium nuclear reactor later this year.[14] In contrast, US deployment has been minimal over the last three decades, with recent large projects facing substantial delays and cost overruns.[31]

此外，中国正在率先部署先进反应堆技术。其“玲龙一号” (ACP-100) 是世界上首个通过国际原子能机构 (IAEA) 安全审查的商业小型模块化反应堆 (SMR)，预计将于 2025 年底并网发电。[31] 中国还在部署快中子增殖反应堆 (FBRs) 等先进系统，并定于今年晚些时候启动世界上首个熔盐和钍核反应堆。[14] 相比之下，美国在过去三十年的部署微乎其微，近期的大型项目面临严重的延误和成本超支。[31]

### 5.3 Government Policy: State Financing and Regulatory Efficiency vs. US Regulatory Friction

### 5.3 政府政策：国家融资与监管效率 vs. 美国的监管摩擦

China’s dramatic success in nuclear deployment stems from a combination of industrial factors that are difficult to replicate in Western economies. These include top-down policy-making, direct public financing from state-backed banks, and low construction labor costs.[14, 31] This approach allows China to build nuclear plants at a significantly lower cost—approximately $2,500 to $3,000 per kilowatt, which is about one-third the cost of recent US projects.[14] Further policies, such as "feed-in tariffs" and Value Added Tax (VAT) rebates, drive the price of nuclear power in China down to about $70 per megawatt-hour, compared to $105 in the US.[14]

中国在核能部署方面的巨大成功源于西方经济体难以复制的工业因素组合。这些因素包括自上而下的政策制定、国有银行的直接公共融资以及低廉的建筑劳动力成本。[14, 31] 这种方法使中国能够以极低的成本建设核电站——大约每千瓦 2,500 至 3,000 美元，这仅为美国近期项目成本的三分之一左右。[14] 进一步的政策，如“上网电价”和增值税 (VAT) 退税，将中国的核电价格压低至每兆瓦时约 70 美元，而美国则为 105 美元。[14]

For the US to regain leadership, analysts suggest adopting a coherent national strategy with a whole-of-government approach. While elements like direct public financing are politically unattractive, the US must learn from China’s success in establishing predictable licensing pathways, healthy support for financing, and a focus on modular construction practices.[31]

为了让美国重夺领导地位，分析师建议采取具有“全政府”方针的连贯国家战略。虽然直接公共融资等要素在政治上缺乏吸引力，但美国必须学习中国在建立可预测的许可途径、健康的融资支持以及专注于模块化建设实践方面的成功经验。[31]

### 5.4 Competition and Geopolitical Linkage: Nuclear Power and the AI Compute Race

### 5.4 竞争与地缘政治联系：核能与 AI 算力竞赛

The speed and massive scale of China’s nuclear energy buildout—across both fission and fusion—is a crucial strategic factor enabling its long-term competitiveness in the AI sector. The escalating demands of AI development require vast quantities of reliable, low-carbon electricity.[6] China’s state-backed model provides a critical energy cost advantage.[14] This ability to deploy stable, cheap power at scale positions China to be the long-term host of the next generation of energy-hungry high-performance computing centers and advanced manufacturing plants, securing a strategic advantage over nations that struggle with higher energy costs and construction delays.[32]

中国核能建设（涵盖裂变和聚变）的速度和巨大规模，是使其在 AI 领域具备长期竞争力的关键战略因素。AI 开发不断升级的需求需要大量可靠的低碳电力。[6] 中国的国家支持模式提供了关键的能源成本优势。[14] 这种大规模部署稳定、廉价电力的能力，使中国有望成为下一代高能耗高性能计算中心和先进制造工厂的长期东道主，从而在与受困于高能源成本和建设延误的国家竞争中确立战略优势。[32]

## Section 6: Quantum Information Science (QIS) (4.1.5)

## 第六部分：量子信息科学 (QIS) (4.1.5)

Quantum Information Science (QIS), encompassing computing, sensing, and communications, holds the potential to be transformational, with quantum supremacy representing a critical national asset.[33]

涵盖计算、传感和通信的量子信息科学 (QIS) 具有变革性的潜力，其中量子霸权代表着一项关键的国家资产。[33]

### 6.1 Research Frontiers: Comparative Strengths and AI/HPC Integration

### 6.1 研究前沿：比较优势与 AI/HPC 整合

The United States maintains a significant advantage in most fundamental quantum research, dominating in foundational algorithms (such as Shor's Algorithm) and demonstrating practical quantum advantages in fields like drug discovery.[34] Federal agencies, including the National Science Foundation (NSF) and the Department of Energy (DOE), actively fund QIS R&D through initiatives like the Quantum Leap and QuantISED programs.[33]

美国在大多数基础量子研究中保持着显著优势，在基础算法（如秀尔算法）方面占据主导地位，并在药物发现等领域展示了实际的量子优势。[34] 包括国家科学基金会 (NSF) 和能源部 (DOE) 在内的联邦机构通过“量子飞跃”和 QuantISED 计划等倡议积极资助 QIS 研发。[33]

China’s approach is marked by centralized coordination and massive resource deployment, resulting in an academic advantage in volume. China now leads the world in the production of quantum computing papers, accounting for 22.8% of global output compared to 21.3% for the US, although US research tends to exhibit a higher impact.[34] China has also demonstrated rapid progress in specific technical areas, successfully actualizing room-temperature time crystals in 2024, an achievement with implications for room-temperature quantum computing.[34]

中国的方针以集中协调和大规模资源部署为特征，从而在学术数量上取得了优势。中国目前的量子计算论文产量居世界首位，占全球产出的 22.8%，而美国为 21.3%，尽管美国的研究往往表现出更高的影响力。[34] 中国还在特定技术领域展示了快速进展，于 2024 年成功实现了室温时间晶体，这一成就对室温量子计算具有重要意义。[34]

### 6.2 Market and Ecosystem: US Private Sector Innovation vs. China’s Centralized Structure

### 6.2 市场与生态系统：美国私营部门创新 vs. 中国的集中化结构

The US relies on a competitive, decentralized ecosystem where private firms, academia, and federal agencies pursue complementary but often independent research agendas, which encourages diverse innovation and market-driven experimentation.[33] This is evident in the progress made by US companies toward fault-tolerant computing, such as Microsoft’s development of 12 entangled logical qubits.[34]

美国依赖于一个竞争性、去中心化的生态系统，私营企业、学术界和联邦机构追求互补但往往独立的研究议程，这鼓励了多样化的创新和市场驱动的实验。[33] 这从美国公司在容错计算方面取得的进展中可见一斑，例如微软开发了 12 个纠缠逻辑量子比特。[34]

China, by contrast, operates a centralized system that aligns science directly with national security goals, translating policy intent into tangible progress across quantum communications, computing, and sensing.[33] China already leads the world in quantum communications.[33] Furthermore, Beijing intends to launch a massive state-led venture capital fund, potentially replicating its successful semiconductor funding model in QIS and other emerging technologies.[33]

相比之下，中国运行着一个集中化系统，将科学直接与国家安全目标保持一致，将政策意图转化为量子通信、计算和传感领域的切实进展。[33] 中国在量子通信方面已经领先世界。[33] 此外，北京打算启动一个大规模的国家主导的风险投资基金，可能会在 QIS 和其他新兴技术领域复制其成功的半导体融资模式。[33]

A significant structural vulnerability for the US is its lack of a robust supply chain and specialized workforce necessary to scale quantum systems.[35] While the US excels in research, it lacks the domestic manufacturing base, precision-component supply chain, and engineering workforce required for moving early systems to utility scale.[35]

美国的一个重大结构性脆弱点是缺乏扩展量子系统所需的稳健供应链和专业劳动力。[35] 虽然美国在研究方面表现出色，但它缺乏将早期系统推向公用事业规模所需的国内制造基础、精密组件供应链和工程劳动力。[35]

### 6.3 Government Policy: The US "Quantum First" Push

### 6.3 政府政策：美国的“量子优先”推进

Given the strategic risk posed by state-supported Chinese quantum programs, a bipartisan federal advisory panel recommended that the US adopt a "Quantum First" national goal by 2030.[35, 36] This goal centers on securing early computational advantage in three mission-critical domains: cryptography, drug discovery, and materials science.[36] Achieving this is viewed as essential for controlling the encryption of the digital economy and gaining asymmetric, persistent advantages in intelligence and targeting.[36]

鉴于中国国家支持的量子项目构成的战略风险，一个两党联邦咨询小组建议美国在 2030 年前采纳“量子优先”的国家目标。[35, 36] 该目标的核心是在三个关键任务领域确保早期计算优势：密码学、药物发现和材料科学。[36] 实现这一目标被视为控制数字经济加密以及在情报和目标定位方面获得不对称、持久优势的关键。[36]

The proposed strategy requires significant policy measures, including increased federal funding for scalable quantum modalities and a mandate to modernize outdated research infrastructure.[36] Crucially, the plan calls for the creation of a new Quantum Software Engineering Institute to coordinate work on compilers, error-handling tools, operating systems, and application libraries.[35] This institutional focus addresses a critical strategic reality: hardware progress alone will not secure technological advantage if the nation lacks a dependable and secure software stack for early applications.[35] The need to shift the workforce pipeline, which traditionally rewards academic research, toward engineering and production is key to overcoming the challenge of scaling early systems.[35]

提议的战略需要重大的政策措施，包括增加对可扩展量子模态的联邦资助，以及强制现代化过时的研究基础设施。[36] 关键是，该计划呼吁建立一个新的量子软件工程研究所，以协调编译器、错误处理工具、操作系统和应用程序库的工作。[35] 这种制度关注点解决了一个关键的战略现实：如果国家缺乏用于早期应用的可靠且安全的软件栈，仅靠硬件进步将无法确保技术优势。[35] 需要将传统上奖励学术研究的人才管道转向工程和生产，这是克服早期系统扩展挑战的关键。[35]

## Section 7: Semiconductors and Microelectronics (4.1.6)

## 第七部分：半导体与微电子 (4.1.6)

The semiconductor and microelectronics sector is arguably the most critical battleground, as chips are foundational components for AI, HPC, and quantum computing.[37]

半导体和微电子行业无疑是最关键的战场，因为芯片是 AI、HPC 和量子计算的基础组件。[37]

### 7.1 Technological Leadership: US Dominance in Design, IP, and High-End Equipment

### 7.1 技术领导地位：美国在设计、IP 和高端设备方面的主导地位

The United States has long been the global leader in semiconductors, commanding nearly half (47% in 2020) of the global market share.[38] This dominance rests primarily on innovation in design, intellectual property (IP), and the ecosystem of leading companies such as NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel.[17, 38] US control over cutting-edge Electronic Design Automation (EDA) software and allied control over extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography equipment (needed for advanced nodes like 7nm and below) represents a critical choke point for China.[9]

美国长期以来一直是全球半导体的领导者，占据了全球市场份额的近一半（2020 年为 47%）。[38] 这种主导地位主要建立在设计创新、知识产权 (IP) 以及 NVIDIA、AMD 和 Intel 等领先公司的生态系统之上。[17, 38] 美国对尖端电子设计自动化 (EDA) 软件的控制，以及其盟友对极紫外 (EUV) 光刻设备（7nm 及以下先进节点所需）的控制，构成了对中国的关键卡脖子点。[9]

### 7.2 Market Dynamics: Global Fragmentation and the Transition to "One World, Two Systems"

### 7.2 市场动态：全球碎片化与向“同一个世界，两套系统”的过渡

The semiconductor supply chain is complex, globalized, and requires massive R&D and capital expenditures.[37] Historically, US design leadership relied heavily on foreign fabrication facilities (fabs), particularly Taiwan’s TSMC, creating a vulnerability that became a national security concern.[38]

半导体供应链复杂、全球化，且需要巨大的研发和资本支出。[37] 历史上，美国的设计领导地位严重依赖外国晶圆制造厂 (fabs)，特别是台湾的台积电 (TSMC)，造成了一种已成为国家安全隐患的脆弱性。[38]

The US-China rivalry, fueled by targeted sanctions and strategic industrial policy, is accelerating the fragmentation of this value chain.[4] The market is moving toward an unprecedented bifurcation, characterized by analysts as "One World, Two Systems".[7] Under this emerging structure, the US sphere focuses on consolidating leadership in advanced processes, securing a more stable supply chain.[7] China, meanwhile, is forced to prioritize the accelerated development of domestic mature process IDM (Integrated Device Manufacturing) capabilities to reduce its dependence on imports (which were only 16% self-sufficient in 2020).[7, 38]

由定向制裁和战略产业政策助推的美中竞争，正在加速这一价值链的碎片化。[4] 市场正走向前所未有的分叉，分析师将其特征描述为“同一个世界，两套系统”。[7] 在这种新兴结构下，美国势力范围专注于巩固先进工艺的领导地位，确保更稳定的供应链。[7] 与此同时，中国被迫优先加速发展国内成熟工艺 IDM（集成设备制造）能力，以减少对进口的依赖（2020 年自给率仅为 16%）。[7, 38]

### 7.3 Policy Leverage: In-Depth Analysis of US Export Controls and the CHIPS Act

### 7.3 政策杠杆：对美国出口管制和《芯片法案》的深入分析

The US strategy utilizes both domestic investment and aggressive export controls. The Biden administration’s 2022 and 2023 controls fundamentally altered the landscape by cutting off China’s access to the advanced chips necessary for training large language models (LLMs) and other AI foundation models.[9] The US also restricted the sale of key EDA software and EUV lithography equipment.[9]

美国的战略利用了国内投资和激进的出口管制。拜登政府 2022 年和 2023 年的管制通过切断中国获取训练大型语言模型 (LLMs) 和其他 AI 基础模型所需的先进芯片的途径，从根本上改变了格局。[9] 美国还限制了关键 EDA 软件和 EUV 光刻设备的销售。[9]

The domestic countermeasure is the CHIPS Act, which provides substantial subsidies to enhance US manufacturing capacity.[4] Crucially, the "guardrail clause" aims to prevent funding recipients from expanding advanced manufacturing capacity in China, thereby securing US leadership and reshaping the geopolitics of the global chip value chain.[4, 11]

国内的应对措施是《芯片法案》，该法案提供大量补贴以增强美国制造能力。[4] 关键是，“护栏条款”旨在防止资金接受者在中国扩大先进制造产能，从而确保美国的领导地位并重塑全球芯片价值链的地缘政治。[4, 11]

### 7.4 Competition and Vulnerability: Huawei’s Breakthroughs and Compute Capacity Disparity

### 7.4 竞争与脆弱性：华为的突破与算力差距

Despite the comprehensive nature of US export controls, strategic vulnerabilities exist, particularly regarding enforcement. In a massive failure in September 2024, TSMC breached export restrictions, producing advanced 7nm process AI chips for Huawei (the Ascend 910B and 910C) through a Chinese proxy company.[5] This violation secured China approximately 3 million chip dies, granting the country computing power equivalent to about 1 million export-controlled Nvidia H100s.[5]

尽管美国出口管制具有全面性，但仍存在战略漏洞，特别是在执法方面。在 2024 年 9 月的一次重大失误中，台积电违反了出口限制，通过一家中国代理公司为华为生产了先进的 7nm 工艺 AI 芯片（昇腾 910B 和 910C）。[5] 这一违规行为使中国获得了约 300 万颗芯片裸片，赋予该国相当于约 100 万块受出口管制的英伟达 H100 的计算能力。[5]

Although these chips lag the US state-of-the-art by about four years, the sheer volume of compute capacity acquired effectively compensates for China’s indigenous production deficiencies in leading-edge nodes.[5] The essential strategic dynamic is that the competition has broadened: the US advantage in the quality of the most advanced AI chips is being offset by China’s ability to achieve substantial, accumulated scale using slightly older, high-volume compute power. This demonstrates that continuous rigorous enforcement and adaptation of export controls are necessary to maintain the intended strategic chokehold.[5]

尽管这些芯片落后于美国最先进技术约四年，但获得的巨大算力规模有效地弥补了中国在先进制程节点本土生产方面的不足。[5] 本质的战略动态是竞争已经扩大：美国在最先进 AI 芯片质量上的优势，正被中国利用稍旧、大容量算力实现实质性、累积性规模的能力所抵消。这表明，持续严格的执法和出口管制的调整对于维持预期的战略扼制是必要的。[5]

## Section 8: Synthesis and Strategic Recommendations

## 第八部分：综合与战略建议

### 8.1 Convergence of Challenges: The Interplay of Technology Domains

### 8.1 挑战的融合：技术领域的相互作用

The analysis across the six strategic domains reveals a profound convergence of challenges, where success in one sector is inextricably linked to stability in others.

对六大战略领域的分析揭示了挑战的深刻融合，其中一个领域的成功与其他领域的稳定性密不可分。

The production of leading-edge Semiconductors (4.1.6) relies heavily on Advanced Manufacturing (4.1.1) equipment, which in turn depends on a secure supply of Critical Materials (4.1.3), particularly permanent magnets used in advanced tools and robotics.[24, 25] China’s dominance in REE refining and magnet production offers a reciprocal leverage point that can be deployed against US semiconductor export controls, creating mutual interdependence and risk.[23, 28]

前沿半导体 (4.1.6) 的生产严重依赖先进制造 (4.1.1) 设备，而这反过来又取决于关键材料 (4.1.3) 的安全供应，特别是用于先进工具和机器人的永磁体。[24, 25] 中国在稀土精炼和磁体生产方面的主导地位提供了一个相互的杠杆点，可用于对抗美国的半导体出口管制，从而造成相互依赖和风险。[23, 28]

Furthermore, the exponential demands of the AI race require immense computational power, making stable, low-cost Energy (4.1.4) the ultimate resource constraint. China’s rapid, state-backed deployment of nuclear fission and its accelerated pursuit of fusion power directly underpin its ability to host future energy-hungry high-performance computing necessary for AI and QIS (4.1.5) development.[6, 32]

此外，AI 竞赛的指数级需求需要巨大的计算能力，使得稳定、低成本的能源 (4.1.4) 成为最终的资源制约。中国快速的、国家支持的核裂变部署及其对聚变能的加速追求，直接支撑了其托管未来 AI 和 QIS (4.1.5) 开发所需的高能耗高性能计算的能力。[6, 32]

### 8.2 Long-Term Trajectories: Assessing State-Led vs. Market-Driven Innovation

### 8.2 长期轨迹：评估国家主导 vs. 市场驱动的创新

The long-term trajectory depends on whether the US’s market-driven flexibility and foundational research strength can overcome its structural vulnerabilities in manufacturing scale and supply chain security faster than China's centralized model can overcome its dependence on foreign IP and advanced equipment.

长期轨迹取决于美国的市场驱动灵活性和基础研究实力能否克服其在制造规模和供应链安全方面的结构性脆弱，且速度要快于中国的集中化模式克服其对外国 IP 和先进设备依赖的速度。

The US excels in flexibility, private sector innovation, and foundational research.[17, 33] However, the difficulty the US faces in restoring deep manufacturing capabilities—the structural hurdle of moving "down the value chain" out of necessity—slows the vital feedback loops between design and production, which are essential for durable innovation.[10, 16]

美国在灵活性、私营部门创新和基础研究方面表现出色。[17, 33] 然而，美国在恢复深层制造能力方面面临的困难——出于必要而向“价值链下游”移动的结构性障碍——减缓了设计与生产之间至关重要的反馈循环，而这对持久创新至关重要。[10, 16]

China’s strength lies in centralized mobilization, which allows for rapid deployment (e.g., nuclear) and massive capital injection (e.g., QIS funding).[1, 31, 33] Yet, this structure creates reliance on foreign IP and equipment [9], and the system's reliance on state control can introduce quality issues and instability, particularly observed in the historic challenges faced by its biotech regulatory environment.[22]

中国的优势在于集中动员，这允许快速部署（如核能）和大规模资本注入（如 QIS 资金）。[1, 31, 33] 然而，这种结构造成了对外国 IP 和设备的依赖 [9]，而且该体系对国家控制的依赖可能会引入质量问题和不稳定性，这在其生物技术监管环境面临的历史挑战中尤为明显。[22]

The policy of technological decoupling carries inherent risks. While essential for national security, overly broad restrictions risk harming US companies by limiting access to large, profitable global markets, potentially damaging the very R&D budgets that sustain US technological leadership.[17] Given the deep, mutual reliance in sectors like biotechnology [19, 20], effective strategy demands targeted, evidence-based containment policies complemented by aggressive and sustained domestic investment programs, carefully calibrating "how much decoupling is good" for long-term national interest.[3, 17]

技术脱钩政策本身带有风险。虽然对国家安全至关重要，但过于宽泛的限制有可能通过限制进入庞大、有利可图的全球市场而损害美国公司，可能会破坏维持美国技术领导地位的研发预算。[17] 鉴于生物技术等领域深刻的相互依赖 [19, 20]，有效的战略需要有针对性的、基于证据的遏制政策，辅以积极且持续的国内投资计划，仔细校准“多大程度的脱钩是有益的”，以符合长期国家利益。[3, 17]

### 8.3 Strategic Recommendations for Sustained Technological Advantage

### 8.3 维持技术优势的战略建议

Based on the comparative analysis of the six core strategic domains, the following recommendations are critical for sustaining and enhancing US technological advantage in the face of escalating competition:

基于对六大核心战略领域的比较分析，以下建议对于在不断升级的竞争中维持和增强美国的技术优势至关重要：

1. **Prioritize Processing Power, Not Just Reserves (Critical Materials):** Policy must focus on redirecting capital and strategic partnerships toward building and securing domestic and allied **refining and chemical processing capacity** for strategic minerals (REEs, Lithium, Graphite).[8] Securing raw material mining rights is insufficient; control over the necessary processing capacity is the true strategic vulnerability.[8]
    
2. **优先考虑加工能力，而不仅仅是储量（关键材料）：** 政策必须侧重于将资本和战略伙伴关系重新导向建立和确保国内及盟友针对战略矿产（稀土、锂、石墨）的**精炼和化学加工能力**。[8] 仅仅确保原材料开采权是不够的；对必要加工能力的控制才是真正的战略脆弱点。[8]
    
3. **Adopt a "Whole-of-Government" Approach to Energy Deployment (Nuclear):** To ensure competitive, low-cost clean energy for future AI and HPC infrastructure, the US must adopt a unified national strategy for nuclear energy.[14] This requires learning from China’s success factors, specifically by streamlining domestic regulatory and licensing pathways and offering attractive financing mechanisms to reduce construction costs and accelerate deployment of advanced fission and fusion technologies.[31]
    
4. **采取“全政府”方针进行能源部署（核能）：** 为了确保未来 AI 和 HPC 基础设施具有竞争力的低成本清洁能源，美国必须采取统一的核能国家战略。[14] 这需要借鉴中国的成功因素，特别是通过简化国内监管和许可途径，并提供有吸引力的融资机制，以降低建设成本并加速先进裂变和聚变技术的部署。[31]
    
5. **Bridge the QIS Engineering and Manufacturing Gap:** To achieve the "Quantum First" national goal by 2030, federal investment must be heavily weighted toward developing the industrialization capacity of QIS.[36] This includes building the domestic precision-component supply chain, modernizing engineering infrastructure, and fundamentally reforming the workforce pipeline to prioritize specialized engineering and production skills over traditional academic research.[35]
    
6. **弥合 QIS 工程和制造差距：** 为了在 2030 年实现“量子优先”的国家目标，联邦投资必须大力向发展 QIS 的产业化能力倾斜。[36] 这包括建立国内精密组件供应链、现代化工程基础设施，并从根本上改革劳动力管道，以优先考虑专业工程和生产技能，而非传统的学术研究。[35]
    
7. **Incentivize Strategic Manufacturing Down-Climb (Advanced Manufacturing):** To reduce critical supply chain dependencies and restore innovation feedback loops, US policy should implement incentives designed to help established firms regain deep manufacturing know-how.[10, 16] This conscious effort to re-engage with the lower ends of the value chain is a prerequisite for long-term supply chain resilience.[16]
    
8. **激励战略性制造业下沉（先进制造）：** 为了减少关键供应链依赖并恢复创新反馈循环，美国政策应实施旨在帮助老牌公司重获深层制造技术诀窍的激励措施。[10, 16] 这种重新介入价值链低端的自觉努力是长期供应链韧性的先决条件。[16]
    
9. **Enforce and Adapt Export Controls Rigorously (Semiconductors):** The efficacy of US technology containment rests on rigorous enforcement. Policymakers must continuously monitor and adapt export control mechanisms to prevent breaches, such as the TSMC proxy failure, which allowed China to acquire massive compute capacity.[5] This focused enforcement must be balanced to avoid overly broad restrictions, which could unintentionally stifle the commercial competitiveness and R&D budgets of U.S. chip leaders.[17]
    
10. **严格执行并调整出口管制（半导体）：** 美国技术遏制的有效性取决于严格的执法。决策者必须持续监控并调整出口管制机制，以防止违规行为，例如允许中国获得海量算力的台积电代理失误。[5]这种集中执法必须取得平衡，以避免过于宽泛的限制，那可能会无意中扼杀美国芯片领军企业的商业竞争力和研发预算。[17]
